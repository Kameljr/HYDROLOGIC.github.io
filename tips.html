Tips to Reduce Water Footprint

    Indoor Tips

    Turn off Faucets While Brushing

    Save water by turning off the faucet while
    brushing your teeth.

    Fix Leaky Faucets

    Repair any leaky faucets to prevent water wastage.

    Use a Dishwasher Efficiently

    Make sure your dishwasher is fully loaded before
    running to save water.

    Install Low-Flow Showerheads

    Replace traditional showerheads with low-flow
    alternatives to conserve water.

    Take Shorter Showers

    Reducing your shower time by a few minutes can save
    gallons of water each day.

    Use a Water-Efficient Toilet

    Consider installing a water-efficient toilet to
    reduce water usage per flush.

    Outdoor Tips

    Collect Rainwater for Plants

    Use collected rainwater to water your plants instead
    of tap water.

    Reuse Cooking Water

    Reuse water from boiling vegetables to water
    houseplants.

    Water Plants in the Morning or Evening

    Watering plants during cooler parts of the day reduces
    evaporation and ensures more water reaches the roots.

    Use Drip Irrigation

    Install drip irrigation systems to deliver water
    directly to plant roots, minimizing waste.

    Avoid Overwatering

    Check soil moisture levels before watering outdoor
    plants to avoid unnecessary water use.
